About Me

I will soon be joining the Department of Second Language Studies at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa as an Assistant Professor.

My research lies at the intersection of applied and computational linguistics, where I explore lexicogrammatical complexity and sophistication in second language production through NLP-based corpus analysis. To support this work, I construct annotated treebanks and evaluate language models for their accuracy in tagging and parsing linguistic features. I am also interested in leveraging large language models to enhance linguistic annotation and analyze learner language.
